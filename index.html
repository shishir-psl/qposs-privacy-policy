Quantum Possibilities Privacy Policy

Effective from 22 July 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and share your information when you use Quantum Possibilities. It also describes how long we keep your information, how we protect it, and how we handle international data transfers.

The Privacy Policy also informs you of your rights and how you can manage your privacy settings.

This Privacy Policy applies to Quantum Possibilities and all of its integrated features, including our social media platform, digital marketplace, wallet services, and crypto-based tools.

At Quantum Possibilities, we want you to clearly understand what information we collect, how we use it, and how we share it. That’s why we encourage you to read our Privacy Policy carefully. Each section includes examples, plain language, and helpful context to make our practices easy to understand.

We also show you where you can manage your privacy settings and data choices directly within the platform. This ensures you have control and confidence in how your information is handled.

13. Crypto Features Disclosure

Wallet Integration

Our application facilitates crypto transactions through WalletConnect, supporting over 400 wallets. Upon a successful connection, the public key is securely stored in our database. Furthermore, users can link multiple wallets to a single QP account.

Token Functionality

Minting & Token Issuance Process

When a user initiates the minting of the Quantum Possibilities Gold Coin, a gold-pegged stablecoin backed by real-time gold pricing, we collect and process specific transaction data to facilitate the minting process securely and transparently.

How Minting Works:

  • Users initiate payment through Stripe or approved bank transfer methods.
  • Upon successful payment, users are redirected to their wallet (e.g., MetaMask) to confirm the on-chain minting transaction.
  • The minting transaction is executed through our smart contract function:

function mintWithPermit(

    bytes32 requestId,

    uint256 amountInWei,

    bytes calldata signature

)

Data we use:

  • Request ID: A hashed identifier (keccak256 of Stripe's payment_intent) is generated to track and validate the mint request on-chain. This does not contain personal information but is uniquely linked to your payment transaction for verification purposes.
  • Amount in Wei: Represents the real-time equivalent value of gold at the time of purchase, plus a platform fee. The current pricing is retrieved via Chainlink oracles. All the prices and calculations will be shown on the screen with platform fee when a user enters the amount to mint.
  • Signature: A digital authorization generated by our backend, proving that the user has completed payment and is authorized to mint. This signature is based on the EIP-712 standard and is signed using the private key of our designated minting authority. 

The signing key used to authorize minting is protected by multi-layer encryption and never exposed to external systems.

Non-Custodial

Our application provides a secure gateway to the blockchain, but we want to be perfectly clear: we are a non-custodial platform. This means we never have access to your funds, your cryptocurrencies, or your private keys. You, and only you, are in full control of your assets.

How It Works: Secure & Direct Interaction

  • Your Wallet, Your Keys: Our app connects seamlessly with leading self-custody wallets like MetaMask, Trust Wallet, and hundreds of others through WalletConnect. You interact directly with the blockchain using your own wallet, which securely stores your private keys. We never ask for your seed phrase or private keys.
  • Gasless Minting (EIP-712): To make minting NFTs or other assets easier, we use a secure method called EIP-712. Instead of you paying a gas fee directly for the minting transaction, our backend pays it on your behalf. You simply sign a human-readable message with your wallet (like a digital signature), which is a gas-free action for you. This signature proves you authorized the mint. It is not a transaction that gives us control over your wallet.
  • Sending Funds (User-Signed Transactions): When you send cryptocurrency, you create and sign the transaction directly within your own secure wallet. Your wallet generates the cryptographic signature (the r, v, and s values), which is then broadcast to the blockchain for verification. This process ensures that no transaction can ever occur without your explicit approval.

Introduction

Your privacy and the security of your data are paramount. This policy outlines what data we collect and why, ensuring you have a clear understanding of how our application operates. Our core principle remains: we are a non-custodial platform and never have access to your private keys or funds.

Data Collection

To provide a robust and seamless service, we collect the following information:

  1. Public Wallet Address (Public Key): We collect and store your public wallet address. This is necessary to identify your account on the blockchain, display your assets, and show your transaction history within the app. This information is publicly available on the blockchain.
  2. Social Media Connection Status: For users who choose to link a social media profile, we store the status of that connection (i.e., whether your account is successfully linked). We do not store personal information from your social media profile itself.
  3. Transaction Event Logs: We record the events related to your on-chain activities initiated through our app, such as mint, send, and withdraw actions.
    • Specific Use Case (Payment Reconciliation): This is particularly important for users who use a service like Stripe to pay for a transaction (e.g., a minting fee). We log the transaction event to verify that the on-chain action was completed successfully after your payment. If the on-chain part fails for any reason (like network congestion), this log allows our support team to troubleshoot and resolve the issue for you.

Use of Collected Data

We use the data we collect strictly for the following purposes:

  • To Improve Your Experience: By associating your public address with your actions, we can provide you with a personalized and accurate in-app experience, such as displaying your transaction history.
  • To Provide Customer Support: Our transaction logs are crucial for diagnosing and resolving user issues, especially for complex interactions involving off-chain payments (like Stripe) and on-chain settlements.
  • To Enhance Security: Monitoring on-chain events helps us identify potential platform-wide bugs or security vulnerabilities.

Third-Party Wallet Integration (WalletConnect)

To ensure maximum security and user control, we have integrated the WalletConnect protocol.

  • This allows you to connect and interact with our platform using over 400 different self-custody wallets (like MetaMask, Trust Wallet, etc.).
  • All transactions are signed inside your own wallet. We do not handle your private keys or seed phrases. The connection simply relays your signed transaction requests to the blockchain.

Security Measures

We employ industry-standard security practices to protect the limited data we store, including encryption for data in transit and at rest. Our primary security feature is our non-custodial architecture, which ensures that you, and only you, have control over your crypto assets.

User Rights & Data Management

You are in control. Within our application, you have the right to:
